Did you know?

"A typical Fortune 500 company that hires 8,000 new employees a year with a 1% gender bias effect can expect 32 additional failed hires and many more sub-optimal hiring decisions, resulting in productivity losses of about $2.8 million per year. A 4% bias effect would lead to an additional 192 failed hires and an additional $17 million in lost productivity." - Michelle Klampe, Writer in News and Research at Oregon State University

Why this tool matters.

Masculine-coded job descriptions lowers job appeal, a feeling of belongingness, and a likelihood to apply for women. A study presented 6 job descriptions to a group of 96 participants. Findings suggest the masculine job descriptions deterred women from applying to those jobs. The reason for this behavior is supported by the fact that the subjects, both men and women, perceived that men would be the ones in those roles. These masculine job descriptions are ultimately telling women they are unfit for the position.

The Pivot.

There were moments when we did not know what to do next, but with enough determination, we succeeded. Initially, we planned to create more of a research tool. We planned to web scrape 16 job descriptions from 4 industries. We wanted to analyze them to determine if they were more masculine or feminine. The web app would include charts with statistics regarding gender in the tech, finance, beauty, and fashion industries. We completed the web scraping portion to the best of our ability.

Then, we then reviewed our objectives and determined that it would be more beneficial for users to understand the gender bias in whichever job description they choose to analyze. Whether you are writing job descriptions or want to learn more about the bias associated with a job you are applying for, our tool will be a step in developing your understanding of gender bias in the workplace.

The Site.

Lastly, we faced some challenges when implementing Flask and developing the website. We had high expectations for what we could do with our web application. We wanted it to be aesthetically pleasing and follow UX/UI best practices.

We struggled initially to develop such a product ourselves. Eventually, we chose to use an existing template similar to what we were looking to produce. We edited the template to our liking and are delighted with the result.
